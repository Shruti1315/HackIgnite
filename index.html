About the Contest

HackIgnite 1.0 is an institute-level hackathon specially designed for the first-year students of MITS DU, aimed at igniting creativity, innovation, and practical problem-solving skills. The contest creates a platform where beginners can step into the world of real-world challenges, learn by building, and gain hands-on experience similar to industry hackathons.

This 12-hour event encourages participants to explore any domain—software or hardware, work collaboratively in teams, and develop innovative solutions that address real-life problems. Problem statements are curated with first-year students in mind, ensuring a balance between challenge and approachability while giving them a true hackathon environment.

HackIgnite aims to help students improve technical skills, enhance teamwork, think creatively, and prepare for future competitions like SIH and national-level hackathons. With guidance, collaboration, and an exciting competitive atmosphere, participants get a chance to turn ideas into working prototypes within a single day.

The event concludes with presentations, judging, and awards including cash prizes, certificates for winners, and participation certificates for all.

HackIgnite is more than a contest—it's the first spark of innovation for every aspiring engineer.

Rules and Guidelines

  1. Each team may consist of 1–3 members.
  2. The contest has two rounds: an online elimination (Round 1) and an on-site final (Round 2).
  3. Languages allowed: C, C++, Java, Python, JavaScript. Any popular language is allowed unless stated otherwise.
  4. Time limits and memory limits will be specified on the problem statements.
  5. Plagiarism or sharing solutions is strictly prohibited and will result in disqualification.
  6. Decisions of the organizing committee are final.
  7. Teams must register using the Google Form (scan QR or click Register).
