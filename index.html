About the Contest

HackIgnite 1.0 is an University-level Hackathon especially designed for the First-year students of MITS DU, aimed at Igniting Creativity, Innovation, and Practical Problem-Solving Skills. The Contest creates a platform where beginners can step into the world of Real-World Challenges, Learn by Building, and Gain Hands-On Experience similar to Industry Hackathons.

This 12-hour event Encourages participants to explore any Domain—Software or Hardware, Work collaboratively in teams, and Develop Innovative solutions that address real-life problems. Problem statements are curated with First-year students in mind, ensuring a balance between Challenge and Approachability while giving them a true Hackathon environment.

HackIgnite aims to help students improve Technical Skills, Enhance Teamwork, Think Creatively, and prepare for future competitions like SIH and national-level hackathons. With Guidance, Collaboration, and an Exciting Competitive Atmosphere, participants get a chance to turn ideas into working prototypes within a single day.

HackIgnite is more than a Contest—it's the First Spark of Innovation for every Aspiring Engineer.

Rules and Guidelines

    Eligibility

    • Only First-year students of MITS DU are eligible to participate.
    • Students from all branches can participate.

    Team Formation

    • Participation is team-based.
    • Each team must consist of 6 members.
    • At least 1 Girl member is mandatory per team.
    • Teams must register with a unique Team name.

    Development Rules

    • One team is allowed to select only one Problem Statement.
    • Students may build Software, Hardware, or Combined Solutions.
    • Pre-Built Projects or Old work cannot be Reused.
    • All Development must be original and completed within the Hackathon Duration.

    Conduct & Integrity

    • No Plagiarism or Copying from other teams.
    • Respectful Behavior is expected from all Participants.
    • Team members must remain inside the venue except during Permitted Breaks.

    Judging Criteria

    Projects will be evaluated based on:
    • Innovation & Creativity
    • Problem Understanding
    • Technical Implementation
    • Practicality & Real-world Impact
    • Team Collaboration
    • Presentation & Demonstration

    Awards & Certification

    • Top teams will receive Cash Prizes + Winner Certificates.
    • All participants will get Participation Certificates.
    • Judges’ decision will be final.

    Miscellaneous

    • Participants must bring their Own Laptops and Required Materials.
    • Refreshments will be provided as per the Schedule.
